Greenhouse Hub

Sharon Ku & Leanne Kate Suen Fa

Project Description

As the perduring pandemic has left many of us feeling more isolated and disconnected than ever, we feel that we need better ways to intimately connect with our loved ones without being capitalized upon and politically manipulated. Therefore with Greenhouse Hub, we want to explore the theme of digital interactions and connectivity in a different way from commonplace social media. By breaking away from the matrix of domination created by big technology companies which dictate how online socializing looks like, we want to reflect on what makes a digital space inclusive and on how we could go around designing a caring online community and environment. As such, we want to reflect on what it means to create a space collaboratively with the users, a space that would be both safe and nurturing for them and which would allow them to be comfortable with being co-creators of this space. Moreover, we also wanted to remove ourselves from the world of intrusive, attention-grabbing notifications, advertisements and otherworldly interruptions and distractions. Therefore, working in an explorable networked space is the basic requirement to create an environment for the cultivation of this kind of connection.

In Greenhouse Hub, the user cultivates precious plants, not in their own but in someone else’s garden. It can be a friend’s, a lover’s, or even a stranger’s. First they choose a seed to gift. There are three types of seeds that can be chosen from and each will bloom into a different plant. Then, by providing dutiful and thoughtful care through sweet notes, photos, videos, memes, gifs, and other creative media, the gardens will bloom beautifully and more plants can be grown. Other options like watering the plant, removing pests, and pollination could also potentially be included to improve the gardening experience. The actions are not designed to be competitive but rather collaborative. The main goal is to connect with each other despite being physically away from each other, share cute moments and show that they think about each other in a peaceful and non-intrusive environment. We want users to feel comfortable and cozy and allow them to completely focus on each other. And of course, we want to foster meaningful and happy relationships over a shared goal of nurturing a plant.

We imagine this world as an infinite explorable three-dimensional space where every user has their own tiny greenhouses called pods hanging magically like a constellation of tiny individual bubbles. The users can navigate through this space (maybe while playing their favorite playlist) and visit each other. Every pod can be visited by the other users although they need to pair up to be able to start growing plants. We want to give users the ability to choose what they want to do in this world, that is, they will need to intentionally want to connect with Greenhouse Hub and their loved ones. There will be no notifications to alert them to come to check it even when they receive new messages. It is only through continuous collaboration and communication that the garden will be healthy and bloom. Plants will have various stages of growth from seed to seedling to young bud to mature blossom. Implicitly, we hope that Greenhouse Hub will also reinforce the user’s connection to nature and hopefully even inspire an interest in gardening through the simulation of cultivating a plant. We also want to empower users to feel good about themselves when they view their progress and in some way give a visual representation of their mutual efforts to develop a relationship with someone else. By providing the option to be able to connect with strangers, we want to promote positive psychology and bring some happiness to others.

Similar Projects

>> Lovebox for Lovers

Lovebox for Lovers is a connected messaging device that allows users to send messages to each other through an app and receive them via the device which looks like a cute box with a lid. A heart-shaped spinner spins in the stead of a sound notification and upon opening the lid, the box reveals an LCD screen displaying the message. The goal of this device is to help friends and lovers connect from a distance through instant expressions of affection like pictures, colourful messages, stickers and drawings. The app also allows the storage of numerous accumulated love notes. Users can also communicate by physically manipulating the box as the rotation of the heart-shaped spinner will result in a waterfall of hearts on the app of the destined loved one. Originally, Lovebox was born in 2015 from the affection and thoughtful attention of Jean who was doing research on robotics at MIT while his fiancee stayed in France. “He wanted to find a way to send not only messages, but his love regardless of the distance” and this led him to the building of the very first prototype. Later on, Lovebox was launched as a startup with the mission of leveraging technology to help people feel and express love and counter the attention-grabbing efforts deployed by “Big Tech” (“Lovebox”).

>> Habitica

Habitica is a free app focused on helping users build habits and increase productivity in a game setting. The inspiration behind the game was born from this question: if people could invest so much time leveling up their characters in games, why can’t they spend the same amount of time developing their real lives? The app uses a social network as a form of motivation by encouraging friends to compete with each other to see who meets more of their self-defined goals. Users can create a party with their friends and track each other’s progress. The game contains spaces for meeting strangers as well, such as taverns, where the user can “rest”, take a break from their daily habits without suffering any health consequences, and chat with other users. They can also join guilds, such as an artist guild, that assigns special tasks and allows users to connect over a similar passion. When the tasks are completed, the user accumulates points that go towards rewards and achievements. These rewards can take the form of a break (the activity is chosen by the user), or achievements that upgrade the user’s avatar. This reward system enhances the experience of completing the task, making the challenge of adopting healthier habits more gratifying (“Habitica”).

>> New Nature by Marpi

New Nature by artist Marpi is a digital petting zoo presented at an art exhibition at Artechouse between October 13th, 2018 and January 6th, 2019. It is the first large-scale solo exhibition of Marpi and also the first artwork displayed at Artechouse based on real-time generated graphics and machine learning. The artwork showcases an immersive digital terrarium filled with nature-inspired creatures and plants which can react and adapt to the visitor’s presence and movements. Thus as viewers engage with the creatures, the artwork responds with real-time computer-generated movements to simulate an organic creature being pet. Moreover, landscapes and creatures evolve, morph and melt as they are engaged with. As they walk through the different installations at this exhibition, different types of movements from the viewers result in different reactions from the digital creatures. In the main gallery, visitors are represented as human-shaped organisms which can interact with a 24ft creature. Their movements forwards, backwards and upwards by jumping translate into their digital representations moving in front and behind the said creature while an app was also created to allow them to feed and play with it. In Gallery One, we see an environment where digital trees awake in response to visitors stepping into the space. The trees then light up and display a colourful show to the viewers. In Gallery Two, gears inspired by succulents respond to the visitors’ movements by twisting and turning on the walls and floor. In the Media Lab, five pods containing several creatures learn and grow using AI and the visitors can use the digital hand to explore the environment and interact with creatures inside it (Marpi).

Comparing Similar Projects with Greenhouse Hub

Greenhouse Hub echoes the goals of Lovebox as they are both designed to foster relationships of love and companionship through sending caring messages between people. However, while Greenhouse Hub’s actions were imagined to only take place in the digital realm, Lovebox’s method of sending messages is dependent on a physical box, one that is quite expensive as well. We aspire to facilitate friendly exchanges in an accessible and affordable way. This entails that it is free to join and simple to navigate. Lovebox uses a heart indicator to notify the receiver that a message has arrived. Our project will not pressure the users to return to the world by concealing all visual notifications outside the website. In Habitica, a community of friends and strangers alike gather in a virtual space to garner motivation to get tasks done. Progressing in the game and unlocking achievements equate to higher levels of productivity. Conversely, in Greenhouse Hub, all the productivity is focused on forming a stronger connection with your podmate and fellow navigators of the world. Furthermore, unlike Habitica’s very structured user interface, our project aims to be much more immersive, visually and sonically. Rather than be constrained to the usual buttons for navigation, we will promote exploration by enabling people to travel in a 3D area. Finally, New World by Marpi exemplifies the liveliness we want to infuse in the plants. Although we won’t go as far as to program them to respond to user movement using A.I., we will make them flourish with the care given to them through user actions. The motion of “growing” a relationship is therefore demonstrated through the visual appearance of the plant.
